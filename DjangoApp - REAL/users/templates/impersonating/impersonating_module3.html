Module 2: Identifying Impersonation Attempts:




3.1 Digital Impersonation Prevention: Safeguarding personal information online, Recognizing phishing attempts and fake websites, Protecting passwords and using strong authentication methods, Reporting suspicious online activities


3.1.1 Safeguarding Personal Information Online:

Limit Personal Information Sharing: Be cautious about sharing personal information online, especially on public platforms or with unknown individuals. Privacy Settings: Review and adjust privacy settings on social media platforms and other online accounts to restrict access to your personal information. Be Mindful of Social Engineering: Be cautious of individuals trying to manipulate you into revealing personal information or accessing your accounts. Be skeptical of requests for personal or sensitive information, even from seemingly trusted sources.



3.1.2 Recognizing Phishing Attempts and Fake Websites:

Exercise Caution with Email Attachments and Links: Avoid opening suspicious email attachments or clicking on links from unknown or untrustworthy sources, as they may lead to phishing websites or malware.

Check Website Security: Before entering personal or financial information on a website, ensure that it has a secure connection (look for "https://" and a padlock icon in the address bar).

Verify Website Authenticity: Scrutinize website URLs, design, and content for any signs of inconsistency or suspicious elements. Fake websites often mimic the appearance of legitimate ones.



3.1.3 Protecting Passwords and Using Strong Authentication Methods:

Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Create complex passwords consisting of a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information or reusing passwords across multiple accounts.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA wherever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts. This typically involves providing a second verification factor, such as a unique code sent to your mobile device.

Regularly Update and Secure Your Devices: Keep your devices' operating systems, applications, and antivirus software up to date to protect against vulnerabilities that could be exploited by impersonators.



3.1.4 Reporting Suspicious Online Activities:

If you come across suspicious online activities or believe you have encountered an impersonation attempt, take the following steps:

Report to Relevant Authorities: Contact your local law enforcement or cybercrime reporting agencies to report the incident and provide them with all relevant information.

Notify the Affected Party: If the impersonation involves another person or organization, inform them promptly so that they can take appropriate action and protect themselves.

Report to Online Platforms: If the impersonation occurs on social media or other online platforms, report the account or activity to the platform administrators.

Remaining vigilant, following security best practices, and reporting any suspicious activities can help prevent digital impersonation and protect yourself and others from potential harm.







In summary, To protect themselves, students should be cautious, verify requests for personal information or financial transactions, use strong passwords, enable multi-factor authentication, and report suspicious activities to their university's IT or security departments.
