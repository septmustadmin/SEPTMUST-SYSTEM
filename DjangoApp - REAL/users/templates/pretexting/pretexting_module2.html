MODULE 2: Pretexting Awareness in Various Contexts




2.1 Pretexting Awareness in Various Contexts: Online interactions (social media, email, messaging apps), Phone calls and voice phishing (vishing)


Pretexting is a deceptive technique used to manipulate individuals into revealing sensitive information or performing certain actions by creating a false pretext or scenario.

It involves the art of creating a believable story or persona to deceive the target and gain their trust.

The ultimate goal of pretexting is to obtain confidential information, such as passwords, financial details, or access to restricted areas or systems.



2.1.1 Online interactions (social media, email, messaging apps)

In the online realm, individuals should be cautious about sharing personal information or engaging in conversations with unknown or suspicious individuals.

Exercise skepticism when receiving unsolicited messages, especially those requesting sensitive information or offering tempting deals.

Verify the legitimacy of the sender or request before responding or providing any information.



2.1.2 Phone calls and voice phishing (vishing)

When receiving phone calls, particularly from unknown numbers, be cautious about sharing personal or financial information.

Avoid disclosing sensitive details over the phone unless you initiated the call and are confident about the caller's authenticity.

Be wary of callers who create a sense of urgency or use intimidation tactics to pressure you into sharing information.
