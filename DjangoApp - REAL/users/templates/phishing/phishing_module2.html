Module 2: Identifying Phishing Attacks




2.1 Common Phishing Techniques: Overview of common phishing methods such as email, social media, and phone-based attacks.


2.1.1 Email Phishing:

Email phishing is one of the most prevalent methods used by attackers to deceive individuals. Attackers send deceptive emails that mimic legitimate organizations or individuals to trick recipients into taking actions that compromise their security. These phishing emails often employ persuasive language, urgency, or enticing offers to prompt recipients to click on malicious links or provide sensitive information.

To identify email phishing attempts, it's important to look out for certain red flags. Some indicators of fraudulent emails include:

● Suspicious email addresses: Check if the sender's email address is misspelled, contains unusual characters, or doesn't match the legitimate organization's domain.

● Poor grammar and spelling: Phishing emails often contain grammatical errors, typos, or awkward sentence structures.

● Urgency or threats: Attackers create a sense of urgency or use threats to pressure recipients into taking immediate action, such as providing personal information or clicking on a link.

● Request for sensitive information: Legitimate organizations usually don't request sensitive information, such as passwords or financial details, via email.



2.1.2 Social Media Phishing:

Attackers also exploit social media platforms to deceive users. ● They create fake profiles or impersonate trusted individuals or organizations to trick users into sharing personal information or clicking on malicious links.

● To identify social media phishing attempts, consider the following:

● Verify profiles: Check for any inconsistencies in the profile, such as a low number of friends or followers, recent account creation, or unusual activity.

● Look for warning signs: Be cautious of messages or posts containing suspicious links or requests for personal information.

● Be mindful of unsolicited messages: If you receive messages from unknown individuals or suspicious accounts, exercise caution and refrain from sharing personal information.



2.1.3 Phone-Based Attacks:

Phone-based phishing attacks, known as vishing (voice phishing) and smishing (SMS phishing), involve attackers using voice calls or text messages to deceive individuals into revealing personal or financial information.

To identify phone-based phishing attempts:

● Be cautious of unsolicited calls or messages: Be skeptical of unexpected calls or messages, especially those requesting personal information or posing urgent situations.

● Verify the source: If you receive a call or message claiming to be from a particular organization, independently verify the authenticity of the communication by contacting the organization directly using official contact information.

2.2 Signs of Phishing: Identifying red flags and suspicious elements in phishing emails or messages.


When it comes to identifying phishing attacks, it's essential to be vigilant and aware of the common signs of fraudulent communication. Some key indicators include:

● Email spoofing: Attackers may manipulate the sender's information to make phishing emails appear to come from a trusted source. Pay attention to the email address and compare it with known legitimate addresses.

● Poor grammar and spelling: Phishing emails often contain grammatical errors, misspellings, or awkward language usage.

● Urgent or threatening language: Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency or use threats to pressure recipients into taking immediate action.

● Request for sensitive information: Legitimate organizations typically do not request sensitive information, such as passwords, credit card details, or social security numbers, via email or other non-secure channels.

● By being aware of these signs, individuals can better recognize phishing attempts and avoid falling victim to them.

Through Module 2, students will gain a comprehensive understanding of common phishing techniques, learn how to identify signs of phishing attempts, and familiarize themselves with real-world examples. This knowledge will empower them to be more vigilant and proactive in protecting themselves against phishing attacks.
